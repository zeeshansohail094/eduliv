The 25 Best Online Masters in Homeland Security Programs

With both domestic and international security increasingly in demand of highly trained, specialized professionals, more and more are turning to graduate programs to gain the necessary knowledge and skills to fulfill both public and private sector needs. Unsurprisingly, the Bureau of Labor Statistics anticipates strong growth across all fields.

Featured Online Homeland Security Degree Programs

  • Colorado Technical University: MS – Homeland Security
  • Northcentral University: MBA – Homeland Security
  • Walden University: Master of Public Administration – Homeland Security Policy

A master’s in homeland security is a broad-based, comprehensive degree, covering everything from terrorism, cyberterrorism, and natural disaster response, among many other areas. Given its range, most programs also offer many customizable options to tailor degrees to fit professionals’ backgrounds and expertise. Here’s our ranking of the top 25 online Masters in Homeland Security programs.

1. Wilmington University

Wilmington University’s online program consists of 33 credits, delivered in accelerated 7-week blocks and other formats, allowing students to tailor course work to their schedules. Three specializations are available: Safety & Security, Organizational Leadership, and Information Assurance. The core curriculum includes Contemporary Issues in Homeland Security, Sociology of Terrorism, Legal Aspects and Policy of Homeland Security, Risk Assessment and Management, Crisis Leadership, and Research Methods. All students also complete a capstone before graduation. With rolling admission and new classes every 8 weeks, students are afforded exceptional flexibility.

  • Homepage
  • Tuition per credit is $444.

2. Arizona State University

Arizona State University offers an MA in Emergency Management and Homeland Security, consisting of 30 credits with a curriculum largely drawn from the School of Criminology and Criminal Justice and the School of Public Affairs. An interdisciplinary, holistic approach combines public policy and management, criminal justice, science, and technology. Students also participate in a culminating capstone project to gain real world experience and apply theory to practice (though no thesis is required). Graduates may complete their accelerated degrees in just one year.

  • Homepage
  • Tuition per credit is $542.

3. Trident University International

Trident University’s 40-credit online program is designed for both military and civilian students with experience responding to domestic crises, terrorist-based events, and natural disasters. Core areas of focus include Crisis Management, Critical Infrastructure Protection, Security Technology, and Intelligence Analysis. In addition, crisis simulations are used throughout the program in order to prepare students for real-world scenarios. For interested students, a Cyber Security concentration is available, in addition to a 16-credit graduate certificate.

  • Homepage
  • Tuition per credit is $510.

4. Colorado Technical University

Colorado Technical University offers a 52-credit MS designed to provide a broad understanding of homeland security on a strategic policy-making level. Core courses include: Homeland Security Fundamentals, Dynamics of Terrorism, Vulnerability Analysis and Protection, Private Sector Role in Homeland Security, and a capstone, among others. Three concentration tracks are available in Cybersecurity Policy, Emergency Management and Public Health, and the general curriculum. For prospective students’ convenience, there are three yearly entry dates.

  • Homepage
  • Tuition per credit is $585.

5 (tied). University of Massachusetts – Lowell

The University of Massachusetts at Lowell offers an MA in Security Studies with a concentration in Homeland Defense, totaling 30 credits. Core classes include Contemporary Security Studies, Scientific & Technological Dimensions of National Security, Intelligence Analysis, and a capstone research thesis. To tailor the degree further, 7 electives are available, as well. In particular, this program emphasizes a “big picture” overview of policy with an interdisciplinary approach on human behavior, systems, and intelligence analysis.

  • Homepage
  • Tuition per credit is $570.

5 (tied). Northeastern University

Northeastern University’s online MA in Homeland Security is a 45-47 quarter hour program – 21 core, 6-8 elective, and 18 devoted to the concentration, of which students choose between Emergency Management and Geographic Information Technologies or Organization and Infrastructure Continuity. Key program objectives are an in-depth understanding of the disaster cycle, the nature and sources of threats to systems, and the development of critical skills, including GIS analysis and communication. The core emphasizes a comprehensive study, with courses including Crisis Communication, Multidisciplinary Approaches to Homeland Security , and Technology and Intelligence for Homeland Security.

  • Homepage
  • Tuition per credit is $615.

7. Drexel University

Drexel University offers an multidisciplinary MS in National Security Management, consisting of 45 credits, which students may take up to 5 years to complete. Drexel emphasizes application of theory with 9 credits of a applied capstone projects required to graduate. Core course work includes Terrorism and Homeland Security, National Security Intelligence, Counterintelligence, and Cybersecurity, Law and Policy, among others. To give students a tailored curriculum, four concentrations are offered: Homeland Security, Intelligence, Cybersecurity, Law & Policy, and Continuity Management.

  • Homepage
  • Tuition per credit is $1,157.

8. Angelo State University

Angelo State University’s online program is a 36-credit curriculum – 18 core, 18 elective. Core classes include Introduction to Geographic Information Systems, Research Methods in Security Studies, National Security Policy, and a capstone. Classes are delivered in 8-week blocks and taught by nationally recognized leaders n border and homeland security, terrorism and human rights, cybersecurity, emergency management, and modern law enforcement techniques and training. Most students complete the program in just 1-2 years.

  • Homepage
  • Tuition per credit is $472.

9 (tied). Mississippi College

Mississippi College’s online degree is primarily designed for part-time students with other professional and personal obligations, and as such is highly flexible and customizable. The core curriculum stresses a comprehensive approach, with classes in Legal Aspects of Homeland Security, Grant Writing for Homeland Security, and Special Issues in Homeland Security, among others. Note also that Mississippi College is a Christian university that promotes and integrates religious values into its curriculum.

  • Homepage
  • Tuition per credit is $545.

9 (tied). American Public University

American Public University’s online MA in Homeland Security is a wide-ranging curriculum that includes studies in homeland defense, intelligence and homeland security, terrorism, consequence management, and interagency collaboration. To customize their course work, students can choose from a number of specializations: weapons of mass destruction, crisis management, narcotics, international homeland security, general national security, terrorism, emergency management, and public health, among others. All teachers are experienced professionals in the field.

  • Homepage
  • Tuition per credit is $350.

9 (tied). Bellevue University

Bellevue University’s online MS in Security Management is a 36-credit track, with an accelerated track available for interested students. Upon graduation, students will be able to identify threats – bioterrorism, nuclear terrorism, cyber terrorism, etc. – conduct vulnerability tests, lead complex organizations, and utilize the newest technologies for analysis. Most students complete course work in just a few years. To real-world projects are used to provide students with valuable experience.

  • Homepage
  • Tuition per credit is $525.

12. Kaplan University

Kaplan University’s online MS in Homeland Security and Emergency Management is a 55-credit program, with 10 elective credits to give students the opportunity customize their course work. Kaplan is especially convenient for part-time students or working professionals, with highly flexible scheduling. In addition, with a rolling admissions and new classes starting every month, prospective students are allowed to begin their degrees whenever works. Core courses emphasize crisis and emergency fundamentals, policies and procedures, and research. A capstone is required.

  • Homepage
  • Tuition per credit is $385.

13. East Carolina University

East Carolina University offers an MS in Security Studies, consisting of 36 credit hours – 24 core and 12 concentration, of which students choose from International Security; Homeland Security Policy; Science and Technology Security; Environmental Health and Occupational Safety. While an internship is required, there are thesis and non-thesis tracks available. All students must pass a comprehensive exam. This program is designed toward working-professionals currently in the field.

  • Homepage
  • Tuition per credit is $835.

14. Northwestern State University of Louisiana

Northwestern State University of Louisiana offers both a thesis and non-thesis track for the MS in Homeland Security – 36-credit and 42-credit, respectively. (Note that the non-thesis track includes a comprehensive exam.) Students complete their degrees in two years. Among the areas of focus include international and domestic terrorism, analysis and identification of terror groups and transnational organized crime, constitutional issues, cyber warfare, cyber forensics, and more.

  • Homepage
  • Tuition per credit is $425.

15. Pace University

Pace University offers an MA in Management for Public Safety and Homeland Security Professionals, consisting of 36 credits and a capstone project. Pace utilizes Blackboard technology, allowing students to learn in a collaborative environment and have access to materials 24/7. Working on a trimester schedule, students complete their degrees in just two years. Core classes include Public Sector Management, Public Sector Strategic Planning and Budgeting, U.S. Constitution and Ethical Issues, and Public Sector Policy Analysis and Program Evaluation.

  • Homepage
  • Tuition per credit is $960.

16. Northcentral University

Northcentral University offers an MBA with a Homeland Security concentration consisting of 30 credit hours – 18 toward the MBA, 9 toward the concentration, and 3 elective. While students may follow an accelerated track, the degree may be completed over 5 years, allowing maximum schedule flexibility. The Homeland Security emphasis includes studies in Homeland Security Transportation, International Crime and Terrorism, Homeland Security Risk Management, and Maritime Terrorism.

  • Homepage
  • Tuition per credit is $850.

17. Virginia Commonwealth University

Virginia Commonwealth University offers an MA in Homeland Security and Emergency Preparedness, consisting of 36 credits. A broad-based, interdisciplinary curriculum focuses on international and domestic security and preparedness issues related to all hazards, including terrorist threats and natural disasters. For interested students, a post-graduate degree certificate is also available, which may be later used toward the full MA.

  • Homepage
  • Tuition per credit is $1,475.

18. Pennsylvania State University

Pennsylvania State University offers a Master of Professional Studies in Homeland Security, consisting of 33 credits. An international track is available, as is a traditional track, which offers specializations in the following 4 areas: Agricultural Biosecurity and Food Defense, Geospatial Intelligence, Information Security and Forensics, and Public Health Preparedness. In particular, this program is designed for working professionals in both public and private sectors. Students can expect to graduate in 1-2 years.

  • Homepage
  • Tuition per credit is $829.

19 (tied). Thomas Edison State College

Thomas Edison State College’s online program is a 36-credit track. Classes are delivered in 12-week formats, and students graduate in just over a year. Core classes include Terrorism and Homeland Security in the U.S., Natural Disasters, Domestic and Global Intelligence for Security Management, and Technology and Information Security. All students also complete a culminating capstone. Students choose 2 electives to complement the core.

  • Homepage
  • Tuition per credit is $520.

19 (tied). Southern New Hampshire University

Southern New Hampshire University offers an MS in Justice Studies with a concentration in Terrorism & Homeland Security. With an accelerated delivery format offered (10-week course blocks), students may complete their degrees in just 15 months. Core classes include Police in the American Experience, Correctional Policy and Practice, and Terrorism and Strategic Practice. Students also choose from a wide variety of electives to further specialize their curriculums.

  • Homepage
  • Tuition per credit is $627.

21. Aspen University

Aspen University offers an MS in Criminal Justice with a concentration in Terrorism and Homeland Security, consisting of 36 credit hours and culminating in a capstone project. Core course work includes Criminal Law and Procedure, Criminology, Major Incident Response Management, and Terrorism and Security Management. An emphasis is placed on specific training and field techniques. (Note that NYPD employees may choose to select from NYPD-specific electives.)

  • Homepage
  • Tuition per credit is $325.

22. Eastern Kentucky University

Eastern Kentucky University offers an MS in Homeland Security with concentrations in Emergency Management and a general concentration. In addition, EKU also offers 2 online associate’s degrees and 4 graduate certificates in the field. Areas of study include border protection, airport security, waterways and seaports, disaster response, counterterrorism strategies and law enforcement intelligence support, and the latest security technology.

  • Homepage
  • Tuition per credit is $600.

23 (tied). Monmouth University

Monmouth University’s online program is a 33-credit track. The university also offers a MA in Criminal Justice with a Homeland Security concentration, and a 12-credit graduate certificate program. Core courses include Homeland Security Intelligence, Cyber Security, Psychology of Fear Management and Terrorism, and Policy Analysis and Research Methods, in addition to a comprehensive exam. Students may complete the program in just one year.

  • Homepage
  • Tuition per credit is $785.

23 (tied). National University

National University offers an MS in Homeland Security and Emergency Management, consisting 54 quarter credits (12 courses), with up to 13.5 transferrable. Core courses include Policies of Homeland Security, Infrastructure Protection, Domestic Terrorism, Emergency/Disaster Response, and Fundamentals of Recovery, among others. In addition, students choose between two culminating capstones. Students typically complete course work in just a few years.

  • Homepage
  • Tuition per credit is $406.

25. Towson University

Towson University offers an MS in Integrated Homeland Security Management, consisting of 36 credits – 15 core, 9 specialization, 6 collateral, 3 internship, and 3 elective. Towson has 3 specialization areas: Information Assurance, Health Preparedness, and Security Policy. With an accelerated course schedule, students may complete in one year, though many students are part-time and complete course work according to their schedules. Note that Towson uses Blackboard technology to maximize learning collaboration and provide 24/7 access to materials.

  • Homepage
  • Tuition per credit is $770.

Methodology:

  • Affordability (1/3): the average out-of-state tuition per credit hour
  • Flexibility (1/3): if the program offers additional specializations and/or an accelerated track
  • Academic Reputation (1/3): the rank of the school’s parent institution according to US News
